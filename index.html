Why Japan is attractive but extremely hard to enter

Japan is one of the most attractive e-commerce markets in the world. High purchasing power, strong online penetration – and very demanding customers. At the same time, regulations, logistics standards and platform rules make Japan one of the hardest markets to enter from overseas.

Japan offers high purchasing power and strong e-commerce penetration, but its regulations, logistics standards, and marketplace rules are among the most complex in the world.

Regulation

  • Pharmaceutical Affairs Act, Act against Unjustifiable Premiums, Electrical Appliance Safety Act, Radio Act, Food Sanitation Act
  • Advertising expressions are regulated even for cross-border EC
  • Risk of account suspension or sales ban for violations

Customer expectation

  • Next-day delivery and high packaging quality are standard
  • Returns happen even when customers simply don't like the product
  • Detailed customer support in Japanese is essential

Platforms

  • Amazon Japan has strict screening and unique algorithms
  • Rakuten requires high operational effort and Japanese-style page design
  • Customer acquisition costs are extremely high with your own EC site alone

Why typical Japan entry attempts fail

Many foreign brands try Japan via cross-border shipping or by signing an exclusive distributor too early. Both approaches usually lead to poor data, low sales and long-term lock-in.

1

Cross-border only: no real market signal

  • International shipping from overseas inventory
  • Long delivery times and not Prime eligible
  • Almost no reviews accumulate
  • Conditions differ too much from normal Japan standards to serve as a valid demand test
2

Exclusive distributor too early

  • Signing exclusive contracts without market data
  • Choosing the wrong partner locks you in for years
  • Sales and customer insights don't come back to the manufacturer
3

Ignoring Japan-specific rules and operations

  • Sales suspended for neglecting Pharmaceutical Affairs Act, PSE, or technical regulations
  • Account troubles from failing to adapt to Amazon Japan and Rakuten operational rules
  • Withdrawal due to underestimating Japanese CS and returns handling workload

Frequently asked questions

Q

Do we need to set up a Japanese company?

No. For the initial test phase, you do not need to set up a Japanese entity. We design the structure so that you can start with minimal fixed cost and flexibility.
Q

Who is responsible for legal compliance in Japan?

We perform an initial risk check and help you design product listings and ads that align with Japanese regulations. However, final legal responsibility always stays with the manufacturer and the importing entity. If needed, we can introduce legal specialists for high-risk categories such as cosmetics, medical devices and foods.
Q

How much budget do we need for the test?

It depends on your category and target, but typically you need initial setup and listing cost, plus a minimum monthly ad budget for Amazon or Rakuten. We will propose a concrete test plan and budget range after the discovery call.
Q

Can you also help us find a distributor if the test goes well?

Yes. Based on the test results and your category, we can support you in designing an exclusive distributor model or approaching potential partners in Japan.
Q

Which categories do you work with?

We mainly work with consumer products that can be sold via Amazon Japan and Rakuten and that can comply with Japanese regulations. High-risk categories may require additional legal review.
